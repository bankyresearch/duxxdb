# Code of Conduct

DuxxDB is built in public so people can contribute, evaluate, and
operate it with confidence. Everyone participating in the project is
expected to keep discussion professional, respectful, and focused on
the work.

## Expected Behavior

- Be direct without being hostile.
- Assume good intent, but accept correction when impact differs from
  intent.
- Keep technical criticism specific and actionable.
- Respect boundaries around security reports and private user data.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, stalking, or sustained disruption.
- Discriminatory language or conduct.
- Publishing private information without consent.
- Public disclosure of unresolved security vulnerabilities.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues, block accounts, or take
other reasonable steps to protect contributors and users. Reports can
be made privately through the project maintainers or GitHub's reporting
tools.
